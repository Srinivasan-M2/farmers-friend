1. Choose the Right Irrigation Method

Use drip for water efficiency, sprinklers for broad areas, and surface irrigation where water is abundant.

2. Irrigate at the Right Time

Water early morning or late evening to reduce evaporation. Avoid watering during hot, windy hours.

3. Avoid Over-Irrigation

Too much water causes root rot and nutrient loss. Only water when necessary based on soil and crop.

4. Know Your Cropâ€™s Water Needs

Water-heavy crops: Rice, Banana. Moderate: Wheat, Maize. Low: Millets, Cotton.

5. Check Soil Type

Sandy soil drains faster than clay. Adjust irrigation frequency based on your soil's water retention.

6. Use Mulching

Apply straw or dry leaves around plants to reduce water loss and keep soil cool.

7. Watch the Weather

Pause irrigation before rain. Use forecasts to schedule watering smartly.

8. Maintain Equipment

Clean drip filters, repair leaks, and check pipes regularly to avoid water waste.

9. Use Smart Tools

Moisture sensors and mobile apps help automate and improve irrigation efficiency.

10. Educate & Learn

Attend local training sessions or KVKs to stay updated on best irrigation practices and government schemes.
